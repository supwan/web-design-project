Global Hunger Statistics

Understanding the scale and scope of hunger worldwide through data and facts

735 Million

People faced hunger in 2023

This represents about 9.2% of the global population, showing the urgent need for action.

148 Million

Children under 5 are stunted

Stunting affects 22.3% of children globally, impacting their physical and cognitive development.

45 Million

Children suffer from wasting

Wasting affects 6.8% of children under 5, indicating acute malnutrition.

Key Facts

  • The world produces enough food to feed everyone, yet hunger persists
  • Small-scale farmers produce 80% of food in developing countries
  • Women make up 43% of the agricultural labor force globally
  • 1/3 of food produced globally is lost or wasted
  • Climate change could increase hunger by 20% by 2050
  • Every $1 invested in nutrition returns $16 in economic benefits
