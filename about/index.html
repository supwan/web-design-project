About Zero Hunger

Understanding SDG 2 and its critical importance in building a sustainable future for all

The Goal

Sustainable Development Goal 2 seeks to end hunger, achieve food security and improved nutrition, and promote sustainable agriculture by 2030.


This goal recognizes that hunger and malnutrition are not just about food production, but about access, distribution, and the quality of food systems worldwide.


The goal is interconnected with other SDGs, particularly those related to poverty, health, education, gender equality, and environmental sustainability.

Key Targets

Support Farmers

Double agricultural productivity and incomes of small-scale food producers

End Malnutrition

End all forms of malnutrition, including stunting and wasting in children under 5

Sustainable Agriculture

Ensure sustainable food production systems and resilient agricultural practices

End Hunger

By 2030, end hunger and ensure access by all people to safe, nutritious and sufficient food year-round

Why Zero Hunger Matters

Human Rights

Access to adequate food is a fundamental human right. No one should go to bed hungry in a world that produces enough food for everyone.

Environmental Sustainability

Sustainable agriculture practices protect our planet's resources for future generations.

Economic Development

Well-nourished populations are more productive, leading to stronger economies and reduced poverty.

Global Stability

Food security contributes to peace and stability, reducing conflicts over scarce resources.
