Causes of Hunger

Understanding the root causes of hunger is essential to finding lasting solutions

Poverty

The primary cause of hunger is poverty. People living in extreme poverty cannot afford nutritious food, even when it's available.

Climate Change

Droughts, floods, and extreme weather events destroy crops and disrupt food systems, particularly affecting vulnerable communities.

Conflict & War

Armed conflicts displace populations, destroy infrastructure, and disrupt food production and distribution systems.

Poor Infrastructure

Lack of roads, storage facilities, and transportation systems prevents food from reaching those who need it most.

Land Degradation

Soil erosion, deforestation, and unsustainable farming practices reduce agricultural productivity and food security.

Displacement

Refugees and internally displaced people often lack access to land, resources, and stable food sources.

Interconnected Challenges

Vicious Cycles

Hunger causes are often interconnected, creating vicious cycles that are difficult to break:

  • Malnutrition reduces productivity and earning capacity
  • Poor health limits educational opportunities
  • Environmental degradation increases vulnerability to climate shocks
  • Conflict drives displacement and poverty

Systemic Issues

Addressing hunger requires tackling systemic issues:

  • Inequality in access to resources and opportunities
  • Poor governance and institutions
  • Gender discrimination limiting women's participation
  • Inadequate social protection systems
